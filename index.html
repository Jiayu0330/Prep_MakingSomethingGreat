  1. Why is visualization effective?

    2. Because "vision is the most developed sense in the human species" (Cairo, 123). In other words, it is easier and faster for human to get the idea if "mapping data into visual properties" (Cairo, 123). (People are too lazy to read text and data:)

    Example: As we can see above, it is definitely easier and more effectiveto read bar chart instead of two columns of data.

  2. What are the more effective ways to encode data for perceptual tasks?

    3. Choose and construct chart "based on elementary tasks 'as high in the hierarchy as possible.'" (Cairo, 129), which means the chart should make viewers quickly and directly get what the author wants to show.

    Example: It is important to choose the right graphic form. Line chart doesn't make any sense here, becuase what we want to see is the mean of each term instead of tendency.

  3. What are trade-offs that Cairo asks us to consider when building a visualization?

    4. We need to consider if we want to show readers big picture or the details. We have to make decision - in general or in detai. If we want to give a general conclusion, we can use area, volume, shading and saturation, color hue, etc. If we want to show some detailed information, we should use bar, line, scatter and so on. For example, if we want to make a choropleth map, color hue works vey well, while our purpose is to reveal geographic clusters. However, we can't give readers specific information by applying color hue.
